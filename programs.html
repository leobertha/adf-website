The Impact We Make

The ADF launched its flagship program, the Amadou Diallo Memorial Scholarship Program, in partnership with the Bronx Community College (BCC), a City University of New York (CUNY) institution two decades ago. With the advent of a powerful Black Lives Matter movement and increased societal focus on racial injustice, the ADF has deepened its impact by expanding its scholarship initiative into a full youth empowerment program to include workforce and leadership development via the following initiatives:

ADF Memorial Scholarship Program

The ADF Memorial Scholarship Program has evolved from a one-off scholarship program to a full academic scholarship that provides mentorship and leadership development from day one through graduation with partner junior colleges in the US.

Submit Application

ADF Computer Center — The Bakeli Partnership

In partnership with Volkeno Senegal, our Bakeli Computer Center program aims to educate 4,000 African youths in web and software development skills over the next four years. A virtual internship program will allow students in our programs (in the US and across Africa) to learn and grow with partner organizations across the globe.

Apply Today

Education For All Campaign

The Education for All program transitions women and girls—particularly in Guinea and Senegal—from just supporting their basic/digital literacy to implementing comprehensive workforce development and entrepreneurial opportunities for the female population in West Africa.

Support Our Sisters

Bridging the Divide Initiative

The ADF promotes global citizenship by connecting its programs and communities of youths in Africa (currently Guinea and Senegal) with the African Diaspora to develop open and resourceful communications. By building a supportive and empowering youth network, beneficiaries of the initiative can be mobilized as change agents for sustainable global development.
